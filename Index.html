Decentralization, Blockchain, and Cryptography

Blockchain operates on a network of computers, reducing the need for central authorities. Transactions within the blockchain are secured using cryptographic techniques, ensuring the integrity and security of the network.

How Cryptography Secures Transactions:

Cryptographic techniques, such as hashing and digital signatures, play a vital role in securing blockchain transactions:

  • Hashing: Each block in the blockchain contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block's data. This creates a chain of blocks, ensuring the integrity of the entire transaction history.
  • Digital Signatures: Participants in a blockchain network use digital signatures to verify their identity and confirm the authenticity of transactions. These signatures provide cryptographic proof that a transaction was authorized by the rightful owner.
  • Public and Private Keys: Cryptographic key pairs consisting of public and private keys are used to facilitate secure transactions. The public key is shared openly, allowing others to verify the sender's identity, while the private key is kept secret and used for digital signatures.

Benefits of Cryptographic Security in Blockchain:

  • Immutability: Once a block is added to the blockchain, its cryptographic hash is unique and cannot be changed, ensuring data integrity.
  • Confidentiality: Private keys ensure that only authorized users can access and control their transactions.
  • Non-Repudiation: Digital signatures provide proof of the origin, identity, and consent of transactions, preventing denial of involvement by any party.
